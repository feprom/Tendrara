Warm-up · From the Fundamentals

Five facts you bring into Unit 200

  • Hydrates: wet gas + pressure + cold → ice-like plugs — the reason E-201 exists.
  • Shutdown hierarchy: PSD (I2) closes process inlet/outlets · ESD (I1) also depressurizes to flare.
  • Gas detection zone 1 covers the inlet: SDV-2001 → SDV-3560 (U200 · 310 · 330).
  • HH / LL process alarms trip automatically — operators verify, they do not race the logic.
  • Plant gas is not odorized — leak detection is by instruments, never smell.
Today these facts become concrete: the valves, loops and trips of the plant inlet.

Process · Stage 2 — Manual §3.4

Heating & hydrate prevention — E-201

  • SDV-2001 — Inlet Skid SDV: isolates the U200 skid for local shutdown / maintenance.
  • E-201: shell-and-tube exchanger, heated by hot oil (from U410).
  • Duty: raise gas temperature → prevent hydrate formation, especially in winter.
  • Control: TIC-2002 regulates gas outlet temperature via hot-oil valve TV-2002.
  • Monitoring: TT-2002 (E-201 outlet) · TT-2001 (feed gas).
  • RD-2005 rupture disk — hot-oil side overpressure protection (position switch ZSH-2005 → PSD).
Hydrates = ice-like water/gas plugs. Prevention here is temperature control, not chemical injection.
Process · Stage 3 — Manual §3.4

Pressure control & filtration — PV-2001 · F-201A/B

PV-2001Inlet pressure control valve — PIC-2001, set point 45.0 barg — stable pressure for downstream processing
F-201A/BParticulate filters, duty/standby — remove solids > 5 µm, protect downstream equipment
  • PDG-2001 — filter differential pressure: rising ΔP = duty filter loading → swap to standby, change cartridge.
  • Each filter protected by its own PSV: PSV-2003A/B, set 50 barg.
  • Site reality: desert dust (chergui wind) makes filter ΔP a daily-watch item.
Process · Stage 4 — Manual §3.4

Final separation — V-201 inlet raw gas separator

  • Final 3-phase separation of the filtered, conditioned stream.
  • Free water → U530 Process Water Package.
  • All condensates → S-413 buffer tank (Hot Oil System) — boiler fuel.
  • Levels: LIC-2001 (light, LV-2012) · LIC-2002 (heavy, LV-2011) · LSLL-2004 independent low-low switch.
  • Outlet isolation: SDV-2002 (HC) · SDV-2003 (water).
  • Overpressure: PSV-2001A/B — set 50 barg.
Gas blow-by: losing the liquid seal sends high-pressure gas down a liquid line — the reason LALL trips close the outlet SDVs.

Operation · Rounds

Daily checks on Unit 200

  • Filter ΔP (PDG-2001) — trend it; swap duty/standby & schedule cartridge change before the alarm.
  • E-201 outlet temperature (TT-2002 vs TIC-2002 SP) — the hydrate watch, critical on winter nights.
  • Corrosion inhibitor XX-201 — confirm injection & tote level.
  • Separator levels V-202 / V-201 steady — watch for water carryover or interface drift.
  • Skid walk: no leaks, no icing, no passing valves · PSV/BDV lines unobstructed.
  • CK checkpoints + shift log — record every reading and anomaly.

Take-home · Recap

Key messages — Module 101

  • 1 · Flow path: SDV-2024 → V-202 → E-201 → PV-2001 → F-201A/B → V-201 → FV-2001 → U330.
  • 2 · Three duties: separate (2 stages) · heat (hydrate prevention) · filter (5 µm).
  • 3 · Loops to know cold: FIC-2001 @ 16.0 MMSCFD (throughput) · TIC-2002 (hydrate defense) · PIC-2001 @ 45.0 barg.
  • 4 · Trips: HH → PSD · LL level → LSD closes that phase outlet — prevents gas blow-by.
  • 5 · Nothing wasted: water → U530 · condensates → S-413 boiler fuel · bypass → E-371 fuel gas.
