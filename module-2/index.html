Block 1 · ESD & depressurization — remove the energy, remove the fuel

Blowdown: the plant empties itself to flare

Blowdown sequenceBDVs fail OPEN · simultaneous per fire zone
  • In a fire, a PSV limits pressure but only depressurization removes the inventory and saves the vessel.
  • Target: 6.9 barg or 50 % of design pressure — whichever is lower — in 15 minutes (API 521).
  • BDVs fail OPEN; all BDVs in a fire zone open simultaneously; rate set by a restriction orifice.
  • No liquid blowdown — liquid inventory keeps vessel walls cool in a fire.
  • NH₃ & mixed-refrigerant loops: PSV only, no BDV (closed loops — ASHRAE 15 / EN 378).
  • Blowdown gas gets very cold — downstream piping is rated for it; solid CO₂ formation is checked.

Block 2 · Energy isolation — LOTO: Golden Rule #3

Lock it, tag it, prove it dead

Nine steps, strict orderone key per lock · one lock per team
Steps 4–5 = isolate every source, then lock + tag. Steps 7–8 = verify zero energy (try-out + voltage-absence test), then earth. Step 9 = authorize — work starts only now, under the PTW.
  • Energy is more than electricity: mechanical · hydraulic · pneumatic · chemical · thermal · gravity.
  • “Tags are warning devices — they do not provide the physical restraint of a lock.”
  • Work on live systems is not allowed; LOTO scope is LV < 1000 V.
  • Major mechanical work: double block & bleed (or valve + spade); actuated valves are not positive isolation.
  • Defeating a safety system = Safety System Defeat Certificate + ALARP assessment (Golden Rule #2).
Only the person who applied a lock removes it. Isolations + LOTO register go into shift handover. (HSE-29)
