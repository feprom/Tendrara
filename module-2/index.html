Warm-up · From Module 001

Five facts you must still know

  • LNG is natural gas held liquid at ≈ −162 °C by temperature, not pressure — volume ÷ 600.
  • Vapour burns only between 5–15 % in air; the liquid itself does not burn.
  • Cold vapour (< ≈ −110 °C) is heavier than air — it pools low and drifts with the wind.
  • Plant gas is NOT odorized — leak detection is by instruments, never smell.
  • Never water on an LNG pool — it accelerates vaporization.
Today we turn these facts into plant systems and emergency response at Tendrara.

Operation · Overview

How Tendrara is operated

  • Continuous, highly automated operation — the PCS holds the process at set points.
  • Operators supervise, adjust, verify — console + field rounds.
  • Independent ESD system protects the plant regardless of the PCS.
  • Fuel-gas autobalance: flash/BOG fully used as fuel or recycled — no routine flaring.

Operating modes

  • Black start — diesel generators from dead bus.
  • Normal start-up → normal operation (design point).
  • Normal shutdown — planned, sequenced.
  • Emergency shutdown — automatic, this module.
Daily reality: monitor the console, walk the rounds, complete checkpoints (CK), load trucks, keep the logs — next slides.

Operation · Access

Plant access & hazardous (ATEX) areas

  • Authorized, trained personnel only — fenced remote site, controlled access.
  • Hazardous area classification (SAF01): zones with possible explosive atmospheres → Ex/ATEX-rated equipment only.
  • No phones / non-Ex electronics inside classified areas; non-sparking tools.
  • PPE on site: hard hat, glasses, FR clothing, boots + cryo PPE & CH₄ detector for LNG work.
  • Grounding & bonding for every transfer operation.
  • Confined/gas-suspect spaces: atmosphere test before entry.

Emergency systems · ESD

Emergency Shutdown System (ESD)

  • Independent safety layer — fully separate from the PCS (API RP 14C).
  • Siemens S7-1500F fail-safe PLC · ET 200SP remote I/O over PROFINET.
  • Inputs: ESD pushbuttons (plant + along escape ways), F&G detectors, critical transmitters, valve feedback.
  • Fail-safe logic executes the Cause & Effect Matrix (SAF12-ING-FR01): isolation · depressurization · shutdown.
  • ESD→PCS link is informational only.
  • Anyone can activate ESD manually on the way out.

Emergency systems · Alarms

General alarm system — know the sounds

  • Siren above the control-room roof, managed by the PCS, UPS-powered — works in a blackout.
  • Differentiated tones for: PSD · ESD · Forced Evacuation · Gas Leakage.
  • PSD: pitched, intermittent — until the parameter is restored & manually reset on the PCS.
  • Forced Evacuation: low tone, intermittent — means leave now to the muster point.
  • ESD: additional pneumatic siren on panel 100-CP-001 — continuous pitched tone, silenced manually once conditions are restored.
Exam favourite: the low intermittent tone = forced evacuation. If in doubt — evacuate.

Emergency plan

Safety & emergency plan — escape & evacuation

  • ≥ 2 evacuation routes from every location, in different directions, never obstructed.
  • Cabins: antipanic doors opening toward escape, never lockable, self-illuminated signs.
  • ESD pushbuttons along the escape ways — activate on your way out.
  • Blackout: control/MCC/2 cabins on UPS lighting + 1 central pole + 3 entrance lights.
  • Safety showers & eye baths in chemical-hazard areas.
  • Layout sized for emergency-vehicle access & turning radii.

LNG emergencies · Spill

LNG spill & vapor-cloud behaviour at Tendrara

SPILLLNG pools & boils — vapour ≈ 600× liquid volume; cold cloud hugs the ground, drifts downwind, lifts above ≈ −110 °C
CONTAINTank & loading-area spills drain to the LNG impoundment basin (NFPA 59A) — away from equipment & people
DETECTLow-temperature sensors — cryogenic liquid is detected by cold, not gas
ACTFoam pourers + deluge activate on the impoundment · shutdown per hierarchy (ASD/ESD)
  • Rollover: stratified tank layers can invert → sudden vapour release — prevented by monitoring & mixing (→ Module 107).
  • The visible white cloud ≠ flammable edge — stay upwind, uphill, and out of the cloud.

Take-home · Recap

Key messages — Module 002

  • 1 · The PCS runs the plant at set point — operators supervise, verify (CK) and log; the ESD protects it independently.
  • 2 · Hazardous areas: Ex-rated equipment only, no phones, personal detector, ground & bond.
  • 3 · Shutdown hierarchy: LSD package → PSD process → ESD plant (depressurize + emergency diesels) → ASD loading bay.
  • 4 · Detection drives automatics: gas → PSD · fire → ESD · cold → foam + deluge on the impoundment.
  • 5 · Alarms: low intermittent tone = forced evacuation · ≥ 2 escape routes · hit ESD buttons on the way out.
  • 6 · Fire logic: never water on an LNG pool · foam controls vaporization · dry chemical for gas fires · don't extinguish what you can't isolate.
