Facility · Safety layer

Emergency Shutdown System (ESD)

  • Independent safety layer, fully separate from the PCS.
  • Siemens S7-1500F fail-safe PLC in the control room; ET 200SP remote I/O near protected units over PROFINET.
  • Inputs: ESD pushbuttons, fire & gas detectors, critical transmitters, valve feedback.
  • Certified fail-safe logic → Cause & Effect Matrix: isolation, depressurization, shutdown.
  • Shutdown levels: ESD · PSD · LSD · ASD (abandon — LNG loading bay).
  • ESD→PCS link is informational only — no safety logic depends on the PCS.
Per the Process Safety & Isolation Philosophy (SAF01-ING-FR01) and IEC functional-safety requirements.
Context · LNG in the world

LNG's position in the world

Liquefaction turned natural gas into a globally traded commodity — moved by sea, not only by pipeline. Tendrara is Morocco's entry point onto that map.

  • ~400 Mtpa global LNG trade (≈2024) and growing.
  • Top exporters: United States, Qatar, Australia.
  • Top importers: China, Japan, South Korea, Europe.
  • Morocco — a new entrant; Tendrara supplies the domestic market.
  • Tendrara ≈ 0.086 Mtpa — a fraction of a single large-scale train.
  • Value here is local energy access, not export volume.

1 · The raw material

What is Natural Gas?

Natural gas is a mixture of light hydrocarbons in a gaseous phase, formed by the decomposition of organic matter over millions of years under high pressure and temperature underground.

Where it is found

  • Gas fields — pure gas reservoirs.
  • Associated gas — together with crude oil.
  • Unconventional — shale gas, tight gas, coalbed methane.

Physical characteristics

  • Colorless & odorless (mercaptan added later for leak detection).
  • Non-corrosive when properly treated & sweetened.
  • Highly flammable within a specific range in air.
2 · Why liquefy?

Why liquefy natural gas?

  • Problem: as a gas, NG occupies an enormous volume — sea shipping unfeasible, mass storage impossible.
  • Solution: cool to −162 °C → it condenses to a cryogenic liquid, volume ÷ 600.
  • Benefits: LNG carriers, mass energy storage, off-grid supply, pipeline independence.

4 · The product

What is LNG?

LNG is natural gas cooled to −162 °C (−260 °F) — the boiling point of methane at atmospheric pressure — condensing it into a clear, colourless, odourless cryogenic liquid.

  • Clear as water, odourless, non-corrosive when treated.
  • Non-flammable as a liquid — only the vapour mixed with air (within limits) ignites.
  • Rapid vaporization on contact with heat (air, ground, water).
  • Not pressurized: kept liquid by temperature, not pressure — tanks are insulated (a giant thermos) at near-atmospheric pressure.

6 · Safety

Primary LNG hazards

  • Cryogenic burns: instant tissue damage on skin contact with liquid or cold vapour.
  • Material embrittlement: carbon steel & common metals turn brittle like glass and can shatter.
  • Vapour cloud: evaporating LNG forms a dense, cold cloud that travels with the wind.
  • Rapid phase transition (RPT): LNG on water can flash-vaporise — keep LNG off open water.
Crucial: LNG does not explode as a liquid — the risk occurs when it evaporates and mixes with air within the ~5–15 % flammable range.
7 · Cryogenic handling

Cryogenic materials & handling

  • Metallurgy: cryogenic service needs stainless steel / 9 % nickel steel (or aluminium); carbon steel is limited to ~−28 °C.
  • Embrittlement: below their limit, common metals shatter — never expose non-rated equipment to LNG.
  • Maintenance: specialized gaskets & seals; controlled cool-down / warm-up procedures.
  • Rapid vaporization: a small liquid release becomes a large gas cloud (~600×).
8 · Safety-critical

Critical safety information

  • Non-toxic — not poisonous like carbon monoxide…
  • …but a serious asphyxiation hazard: it displaces oxygen.
  • Oxygen-deficient atmospheres (< 19.5 %) can be fatal — the top risk in confined spaces during maintenance.
  • Odorization: household gas has mercaptan added so leaks can be smelled.
  • In LNG plants the gas is NOT odorized.
  • ➜ Safety depends on instrumentation & gas detectors — never on smell.
Before entering any confined or gas-suspect area: test the atmosphere. Trust the detector, not your nose.
9 · Plant safety framework

Process Safety & Isolation Philosophy

  • Layers of protection: PCS control → independent ESD → relief & depressurization to flare.
  • Shutdown hierarchy: LSD (local) → PSD (process) → ESD (emergency) → ASD (loading bay), per the Cause & Effect Matrix.
  • Relief & depressurization: PSVs & blowdown valves (BDV) route to the flare in upsets.
  • Isolation for maintenance: positive isolation, double-block-and-bleed, car-sealed / locked valves (CSO/CSC, LO/LC), vessel-entry isolation.
  • Personnel safeguards: emergency showers & eye baths, F&G detection, high-expansion foam for cryogenic spills.
Source: SAF01-ING-FR01 Rev.2 — Process Safety & Isolation Philosophy (Italfluid). Standards: NFPA 59A · EN 1473 · IEC 60079 (Ex).
10 · Uses

Applications of NG & LNG

  • Power: combined-cycle plants, gas turbines, industrial engines — lower CO₂ than coal/oil.
  • Industry: furnaces, boilers, dryers, precise thermal processes.
  • Feedstock: ammonia (fertilizers), methanol (plastics, resins).
  • Transport: CNG for urban fleets; LNG for heavy-duty trucking & marine.
  • Off-grid / island supply & virtual pipelines — Tendrara's role for Morocco.
Take-home

Key technical messages

  • 1 · LNG — liquid due to extreme temperature (−162 °C), not pressure.
  • 2 · CNG — reduces volume through high pressure, not cooling.
  • 3 · Pipeline NG — depends entirely on fixed physical infrastructure.
  • 4 · Hazard by state — NG: explosive/fire · LNG: cryogenic + vapour cloud · CNG: mechanical energy.
  • 5 · Odorization — in industrial & LNG plants the gas is NOT odorized.
  • 6 · Safety depends on instrumentation & gas detectors — never on smell.
