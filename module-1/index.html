1 · The product

What is LNG?

Liquefied Natural Gas is natural gas — mostly methane (CH₄) — purified and cooled to about −162 °C at near-atmospheric pressure, condensing it into a clear, colourless, odourless cryogenic liquid.

  • Liquefaction shrinks the volume ~600:1 → economic to store & truck where pipelines don't reach.
  • LNG is not toxic and not corrosive. Its hazards: extremely cold, an asphyxiant, flammable once re-vaporised.
  • The liquid does not burn — only the vapour burns, between ~5 % and ~15 % in air.
  • Tendrara's purpose: monetise field gas and supply LNG off-grid by road tanker.

6 · The facility

Plant overview

A modular, skid-mounted gas treatment and liquefaction facility that processes raw gas from wells TE-6 and TE-7 and produces specification-grade LNG for road-tanker export, engineered to run reliably in a remote field environment.

  • Compact footprint, high modularization, factory-tested skids; field connections at the skid edge.
  • Fully integrated scheme: pretreatment → liquefaction → LNG handling.
  • Each step protects downstream equipment, maintains product quality, respects cryogenic limits.
  • Layout designed for continuous operation with safe, controlled shutdowns.

9 · Safety

Principal LNG hazards

  • Cryogenic (cold): −162 °C causes severe cold burns and embrittles unsuitable metals.
  • Asphyxiation: vaporising LNG displaces oxygen in low/confined areas — odourless.
  • Flammable vapour cloud: a warming cloud within 5–15 % can flash-fire to the pool.
  • Spill events: RPT (LNG on water), rollover (tank stratification), BLEVE (vessel rupture).
Reminder: the liquid itself doesn't burn — control the vapour and keep LNG off open water.
Hazardous areas

Ex / ATEX areas & European standards

  • Area classification: Zone 0 / 1 / 2 — where a flammable gas atmosphere may be present.
  • Ex / ATEX-marked equipment is certified for explosive atmospheres — mandatory in classified zones.
  • ATEX 2014/34/EU (equipment) & 1999/92/EC (workplaces).
  • IEC/EN 60079 explosive atmospheres · EN 1473 LNG installations · PED 2014/68/EU pressure equipment.
Ex mark — equipment for potentially explosive atmospheres
European set complements NFPA 59A (US). Plant hazardous-area zoning: see the Tendrara area-classification drawings.
10 · Safeguards

Precautions & safety systems

  • Shutdown hierarchy: LSD (local) → PSD (process) → ESD (emergency, with blowdown to flare).
  • Isolation philosophy: skid-edge isolation, car-sealed / locked valves, vessel-entry isolation.
  • Fire & Gas (Unit 700): gas, flame & low-temp detectors → ESD logic.
  • Relief & flare (Unit 230): PSVs & blowdown valves depressurise to flare in upsets.
  • High-expansion foam (Unit 730): blankets a cryogenic spill, suppresses the cloud.
  • Containment & utilities: bunds/closed drain (550), N₂ purge (600), emergency showers & eye baths.
Per Process Safety & Isolation Philosophy (SAF01-ING-FR01). Standards: NFPA 59A · EN 1473 · GIIGNL.
